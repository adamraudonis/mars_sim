# ECLSS / Life Support — Parameter Database

Domain key: `eclss`
Scope: crew metabolic loads, cabin atmosphere setpoints, habitat leakage, and ISS-heritage
regenerative ECLSS hardware performance (O2 generation, CO2 removal, CO2 reduction, water
recovery) for a Mars habitat/settlement trade-study model.

Primary source of record for human interface values is the **NASA Life Support Baseline Values
and Assumptions Document (BVAD), NASA/TP-2015-218570 Rev 2, Feb 2022** (NTRS accession
20210024855). Hardware performance is drawn from ISS flight-heritage ICES papers and NASA
technical briefs. All company/agency claims are dated.

Unit conventions: SI. Energy in kWh; time in sols where natural
(1 sol = 88 775 s = 24.6597 h; 1 Mars year = 668.6 sols). "CM" = crew member.
Pressure conversions used: 1 mmHg = 0.13332 kPa; 1 psi = 6.8948 kPa; 1 torr = 1 mmHg.

### 1.2 BVAD Table 4-1 — design ranges (lower / nominal / upper)

Table 4-1 gives the ranges an ECLSS should be sized against; these differ slightly from the
single-point Table 3-31 balance because they fold in activity/metabolism spread.

| Parameter | Units | Lower | Nominal | Upper |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CO2 generated | kg/CM-d | 0.74 | 1.08 | 1.31 |
| O2 consumed | kg/CM-d | 0.61 | 0.90 | 1.08 |

|---|---|---|---|

| Total pressure — reduced options | kPa | **70.3** (10.2 psia) or **56.5** (8.2 psia) | EVA-prebreathe-reduction atmospheres |

| ppCO2 — NASA-STD-3001 limit (1-h avg) | kPa | **0.40** (3 mmHg) | [V2 6004]; headache incidence < 1.4 % |
| ppCO2 — 7-day target for < 1 % headache | kPa | 0.33 (2.5 mmHg) | OCHMO-TB-004 |
| ppCO2 — BVAD design nominal | kPa | 0.507 | Table 4-1 (older 0.5-kPa design point) |
| ppCO2 — ISS observed operational band | kPa | 0.13–1.2 (1–9 mmHg) | OCHMO-TB-004 |
| Cabin temperature | K | 291 / **296** / 300 | ≈ 18–27 °C |
| Relative humidity | % | 25 / **40** / 75 | |

The **8.2 psia / 34 % O2 exploration atmosphere** (56.5 kPa) is NASA's leading option for surface
habitats/suits because it cuts EVA prebreathe time while keeping ppO2 ≈ 19–21 kPa and staying
below the flammability ceiling (~30 % O2 fraction is the usual working limit at 101 kPa; higher
O2 fractions are only acceptable at reduced total pressure). SMAC note: NASA has **deleted the
formal CO2 SMAC** — CO2 is now managed against NASA-STD-3001 performance/health-decrement
limits rather than a toxicological SMAC.

## 5. ISS-heritage regenerative ECLSS hardware

### 5.1 Oxygen Generation Assembly (OGA / OGS) — PEM water electrolysis

| Parameter | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Max O2 production | **9.25 kg/day** (20.4 lb/day) at 46.9 A stack current | ICES-2023-311 (NTRS 20230008013) |
| Crew supported at max | **10.88 CM** (@ 0.85 kg O2/CM-d) | ICES-2023-311 |
| Stack current range | 1.0 A (standby) → 10–46.9 A (process) | ICES-2023-311 |
| Nominal cell voltage | ~1.7 V/cell | ICES-2023-311 |
| Derived stack size | ≈ 28 cells (Faradaic derivation, see below) | derived |
| Derived stack power at max | ≈ 2.23 kW | derived |
| **Stack specific energy** | **≈ 5.8 kWh/kg O2** | derived (see below) |
| System specific energy (incl. BoP, part-load) | ~7–12 kWh/kg O2 | estimate |
| Cumulative production (2007–Mar 2023) | 11 903 kg O2, 1 488 kg H2 | ICES-2023-311 |
| Life-limited ORUs | H2 sensor 201 d; ACTEX 675 d; inlet DI bed ~8 yr | ICES-2023-311 |

**Faradaic derivation of stack size / specific energy** (physics check, L2):
O2 molar rate at 9.25 kg/day = 9250 g ÷ 32 g/mol ÷ 86 400 s = 3.347×10⁻³ mol/s.
Water electrolysis needs 4 electrons per O2. In a series stack the same current I flows through
all N cells and each produces O2, so N = (ṅ_O2 · 4 · F)/I = (3.347×10⁻³ · 4 · 96 485)/46.9 ≈ **28
cells**. Stack voltage = 28 × 1.7 V = 47.6 V; stack power = 47.6 × 46.9 = **2.23 kW**.
Specific energy = 2.23 kW × 24 h ÷ 9.25 kg = **5.8 kWh/kg O2** at the stack.
(Thermoneutral minimum for water electrolysis is ~4.97 kWh/kg O2 at 1.48 V/cell; the 1.7 V
operating point gives ~16 % voltage overhead, consistent with 5.8 kWh/kg.)

ISS historical 1–9 mmHg band that caused crew headaches.

### 5.5 Trace Contaminant Control (TCCA) and atmosphere monitoring

TCCA uses activated charcoal + a high-temperature catalytic oxidizer + LiOH post-sorbent to
control VOCs (methane, CO, H2, ammonia, formaldehyde, etc.). BVAD Table 4-3 gives the trace
contaminant generation model with metabolic + equipment source rates and SMACs, e.g. ammonia
50 mg/CM-d (SMAC 2 mg/m³), CO 18 mg/CM-d (SMAC 17 mg/m³), acetone 19 mg/CM-d, methanol
0.9 mg/CM-d. Major Constituent Analyzer (mass spectrometer) monitors ppN2/O2/CO2/CH4/H2/H2O.

### 5.6 Nitrogen makeup

Cabin gas is ~78 % N2 by mole in the 101.3 kPa atmosphere (ppN2 ≈ 79 kPa). N2 is essentially
non-consumed — makeup is required only to replace leakage (~0.02 kg/day/module of mixed gas,
of which ~0.75 by mass is N2) plus airlock/EVA depressurization losses. On ISS, N2 is delivered
from high-pressure tanks (recharge ≤ 3400 psia, ≤ 3 lb/h) and O2 from tanks/OGA (≤ 1050 psia,
≤ 16 lb/h). For a Mars habitat, N2 (or a buffer gas) is a slow-consumable dominated by airlock
cycling, not metabolism.

## 6. ISS on-orbit reliability context

- **OGA:** >15 years operating; dominant failure modes are cell-stack membrane thinning/low-cell-
  voltage, pump lockups (nickel-phosphate precipitation on gears in storage), H2-sensor drift,
  and recirculation-loop filter clogging from pH excursions. Most ORUs run-to-failure; H2 sensor
  is calibration-life-limited (201 days).
- **CDRA:** operational problems since Feb 2001 — sticking/failing air-select valves, sorbent
  dusting, and pump anomalies; two rack units flown for redundancy.
- **UPA:** urine recovery de-rated from 85 % to ~70 % after calcium-sulfate precipitation fouled
  the distillation assembly; pretreatment chemistry is the persistent constraint.
- Implication for Mars: for a 3-year mission with no resupply, spares mass and crew maintenance
  time dominate ECLSS logistics; reliability/redundancy, not steady-state efficiency, is the
  binding trade. (NTRS 20190027319 "High Reliability Requires More Than Providing Spares".)

## 7. Fidelity tiers

### L0 — single scalar averages (mass-balance sizing)
Per crew member, constant:
- O2 consumed 0.90 kg/CM-d; CO2 produced 1.04 kg/CM-d (RQ 0.86).
- Potable water 2.5 kg/CM-d; hygiene 0.4–2.0 kg/CM-d (mission-class dependent).
- Urine 1.5 kg/CM-d; humidity condensate 2.27 kg/CM-d.
- Metabolic heat 143.8 W (12.43 MJ/CM-d), sensible/latent split 51 %/49 %.
- Leakage 0.02 kg/day/module (or 0.05–0.14 %/day of cabin gas mass).
- Closure: O2 loop via OGA + Sabatier → net O2 resupply = 0.90 × (1 − 0.48) ≈ 0.47 kg/CM-d unless
  methane pyrolysis added; water loop 98 % closed → net water resupply ≈ 0.02 × (potable+hygiene).
Governing form: `resupply_i = load_i × (1 − recovery_i) × N_crew × mission_sols`.

### L1 — analytic daily / seasonal model
- Split crew day into activity blocks (sleep / nominal / exercise / EVA) with block-specific
  metabolic rates and RQ (0.86 rest → 0.95–0.96 exercise); integrate O2/CO2/heat over the sol.
  ISS reference schedule ≈ 8 h sleep, ~1.5 h exercise, rest nominal.
- Atmosphere state as ODEs over a sol with slowly varying setpoints:
  - CO2: `V·dρCO2/dt = ṁ_CO2,crew − CDRA_removal(ppCO2)`, CDRA capacity a function of inlet ppCO2
    (4.1 CM-eq @ 2 torr, 10.5 CM-eq @ 5 torr — roughly linear in ppCO2 over 2–5 torr).
  - O2: `V·dρO2/dt = OGA_prod − ṁ_O2,crew − leak_O2`.
  - Total pressure/leak: `dm_gas/dt = −k_leak·m_gas` with k_leak ≈ 0.0005–0.0014 /day.
- Power as a function of production rate: OGA ≈ 5.8 kWh/kg O2 (stack) + fixed BoP; CDRA duty-cycled.
- Water inventory with per-stream recovery fractions and expendable-bed depletion (WPA beds
  ~472 kg/yr for 6 crew ⇒ periodic replacement events).

### L2 — physics-based sub-sol timestep model
State vector per module: {p_total, ppO2, ppCO2, ppN2, pH2O(RH), T_cabin, m_water_nodes,
sorbent_loadings}. Timestep ~minutes.
- **Atmosphere species (per gas i):**
  `d(ppᵢ)/dt = (R·T/V)·[ ṅᵢ,sources − ṅᵢ,sinks(ppᵢ, hardware state) − ṅᵢ,leak ]`,
  with `ṅᵢ,leak = ppᵢ/p_total · k_leak · n_total` (composition-proportional leakage).
- **CDRA:** dual-bed adsorption with Langmuir/Toth CO2 isotherms on 13X/5A vs temperature and
  ppCO2, thermal-swing regeneration cycle (half-cycle time, heater ramp), so removal rate is
  transient and duty-cycle dependent rather than a constant kg/day.
- **OGA:** electrolysis current I sets ṅ_O2 = N_cells·I/(4F) and ṅ_H2 = 2·ṅ_O2; stack power
  P = N_cells·V_cell(I)·I with V_cell(I) = 1.23 + η_act + η_ohmic·I (polarization curve).
- **Sabatier:** equilibrium-/kinetics-limited CO2+4H2→CH4+2H2O with H2 availability = OGA H2
  output; unrecovered H2 (in CH4) sets the ≤50 % O2 closure ceiling unless a CH4 pyrolysis node
  is added to recycle H2.
- **Humidity/thermal:** condensing heat exchanger removes latent load (6.12 MJ/CM-d) at the CHX;
  sensible load (6.31 MJ/CM-d) to the cabin air loop → couples to the thermal-control domain.
- **Water network:** nodal inventory (potable, waste, urine, brine, condensate) with UPA (85 %
  urine, de-ratable), WPA (~93 %+), BPA (brine → 98 % overall) transfer efficiencies and
  expendable-bed capacity states.

Cross-domain couplings: sensible/latent heat → thermal domain; OGA/CDRA/Sabatier electrical
draw → power domain; O2/N2/water/spares resupply mass → logistics/ISRU domain (Mars ISRU can
supply O2 and N2/buffer gas locally, changing the closure economics vs ISS).

## 8. Key disagreements / caveats between sources

1. **O2 & CO2 per crew:** BVAD Table 3-31 (0.895 O2 / 1.085 CO2) vs Table 4-1 nominal (0.90 /
   1.08) vs the widely quoted legacy 0.816/1.0 kg/CM-d. Use ~0.90/1.04 and carry ±25 % spread.
2. **UPA recovery:** design 85 % vs de-rated 70 % on orbit (Ca-sulfate precipitation). Model
   should treat urine recovery as a de-ratable parameter, not a fixed 85 %.
3. **WRS 98 %:** this is a **2023 demonstration with BPA**, not routine steady state everywhere;
   93–94 % is the pre-BPA baseline. Treat 98 % as best-case closed-loop, 93 % as conservative.
4. **CDRA power:** open literature gives capacity and flow cleanly but not a single clean nominal
   power; ~0.6–1.0 kW is an engineering estimate (heaters dominate, +200 W at high-flow config).
5. **Sabatier closure:** "~50 %" is an upper bound; ISS estimated ~47 %. >50 % O2 closure requires
   methane management not yet operational.
6. **Leakage %/day:** BVAD kg/day/module vs %/day depends on assumed module free volume; convert
   using resident gas mass for the specific habitat.
